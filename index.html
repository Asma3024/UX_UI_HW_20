Kids Against Hunger Canada

A Nonprofit Website Redesign

To help increase contributions by improving transparency in the donation process, we redesigned Kids Against Hunger Canada's website from the user perspective

  • The Process
  • User Research
  • Ideation

MY ROLE & RESPONSIBILITIES

In this group project, I have been responsible to

  • Lead the user research process, create empthy map, ideation, and participate in information architecture
  • Plan, prepare, conduct user interviews and a survey
  • Conduct usability testing of the prototype, final product, A/B testing, and communicate the key findings to the team members

TIMELINE

It was a three week long project

USER RESEARCH

To understand the process, goals, motivations and pain points people had while visiting a nonprofit website. I interviewed the

stakeholder and other current and potential users around Toronto. I also conducted a surveys.

EMPATHY MAP & USER PERSONA

The interview and survey insights helped us create an empathy map. We found common pain points among users when donating to non-profit websites, which led us to develop our user persona, Sarah.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

IDEATION

After the competitor analysis, the team participated in the ideation process and through several brainstorming sessions, created a feature priority matrix to solve the defined problem.

CARD SORTING & SITEMAP

The ideation phase, lead us to Information Architecture phase, in which we did card sorting to create a new sitemap for the redesign.

WIREFRAMING

After we agreed on our redesign ideas, low and mid fidelity wire frames for desktop and mobile versions were created.

USABILITY TESTING

Before moving forward to create Hi- fidelity prototype, we made a usability plan and tested our mid- fidelity prototype for desktop and mobile versions on the users. A/B testing was also conducted for left or middle alignment of the text in the mobile version.

STYLE GUIDE

The usability testing helped the team to iterate the card sorting and sitemap, and we were ready to create the style guide to add UI elements to our Hi- Fidelity prototype.
